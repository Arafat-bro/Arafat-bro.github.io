Interests

My research interests lie in the fields of plant physiology, plant-pest and disease ecology, and plant-soil interactions, focusing on understanding how plants respond to environmental stresses and biotic challenges. I am particularly interested in exploring physiological mechanisms that enhance plant resilience, as well as the ecological dynamics between crops, pests, and pathogens. Additionally, I aim to investigate the complex interactions between plants and soil, including nutrient uptake efficiency and microbial associations, to improve sustainable agricultural practices.

Furthermore, my interests extend to crop genetics and the application of nanotechnology in agriculture. I am keen on studying genetic improvements in crops to enhance yield, stress tolerance, and disease resistance through modern breeding techniques. Additionally, I am intrigued by the potential of nanotechnology in agriculture, particularly in precision farming, targeted nutrient delivery, and pest management. By integrating these fields, I hope to contribute to sustainable and innovative solutions for global agricultural challenges.
